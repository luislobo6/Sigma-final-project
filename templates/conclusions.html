Over the past decade there has been a clear dominance in the banking system by 4 main banks Bancomer now BBVA, Citibanamex, Santander and each year more aggressively local bank Banorte.

As history goes until 2010 Citibanamex was the biggest bank in mexico until BBVA (then Bancomer) surpass it in 2011 and then in 2015 and 2017 Santander and Banorte respectively in general terms. It can be said that BBVA is the clear leader amongst the 4 biggest banks and the other 3 compete in different products for the second place but hold a similar portion of the market in general

Although there are banking licenses for more than 40 banks with the next tier of banks (Inbursa, HSBC, Scotia, Azteca, Bajio and Banregio) Mexico concentrates 86% of its banking income in 10 banks (60% in the big 4)

Lending market has been decreasing mainly due to covid closings and credit tightening restrictions. It is clear that the international banks reduce their risk appetite with Citibanamex showing a 15%+ reduction YOY on their lending. Banorte and the other local banks such as Azteca and Bajio have grown during pandemic period

In terms of deposits we also saw a higher than expected growth mainly due to closing of commercial activity that reduce the places for the general public to spend and reduce the investment for companies that preferred to maintain a long liquidity position

In terms of profitability 61% of revenues are produced in by the biggest four bank however they represent 77% of system EBIT as the spaniards and Banorte efficiency are much better than the system. Citibanamex does not follow the trend and with a 14% share in revenues only contributed with 8% of the Earnings before tax

From ML fbprophet

The interesting part about the prophet projections is that banks can dimension the long term size of the market after a recovery period. Managing risk will determine which bank will gain the most market share after the economic cycle restarts and the growth path returns to Mexican market

For the prophet projections we use data from 2000 until 2018 to train the machine learning model. We use 2019 as a test determining that the model was in deed showing an accurate projection, none the less for 2020 in all trained models the behavior was far off from expectations, in Deposits for instance with a higher than expected growth and for lending there is a clear reversion on the growth trend showing an actual decrease YOY from April onwards.
